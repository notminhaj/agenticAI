### 1) [2504.16153v1] Leveraging Social Media Analytics for Sustainability Trend Detection in Saudi Arabias Evolving Market
https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.16153v1

**Summary:** **Problem:** The paper addresses the need for real-time detection of sustainability trends in Saudi Arabia, driven by the country's rapid economic growth and social evolution under Vision 2030. Understanding these trends can unlock new business and investment opportunities.

**Approach:** The author employs AI-driven social media analytics to process millions of posts, news articles, and blogs to identify and monitor sustainability trends across various sectors, including construction, food and beverages, tourism, technology, and entertainment.

**Key Results:** The findings provide sector-specific and cross-sector insights, enabling economists, businesses, and government entities to make informed decisions based on a reliable snapshot of market shifts in Saudi Arabia. The methodology also shows adaptability for use in other regions.

**Why It Matters:** This research highlights the potential of AI to enhance decision-making by providing insights into public perceptions and the adoption of sustainability initiatives. By leveraging social media analytics, stakeholders can better understand and respond to evolving market trends, ultimately contributing to sustainable development in Saudi Arabia and beyond.

### 2) [2502.16871v1] Utilizing Social Media Analytics to Detect Trends in Saudi Arabias Evolving Market
https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.16871v1

**Summary:** **Summary of Paper: "Utilizing Social Media Analytics to Detect Trends in Saudi Arabia's Evolving Market"**

**Problem:** The rapid economic growth and societal changes in Saudi Arabia under Vision 2030 create a need for businesses to identify and track emerging market trends effectively.

**Approach:** The paper employs an AI-driven methodology to analyze millions of social media posts monthly, classifying discussions and scoring them to reveal both emerging and diminishing trends across various sectors, including construction, food and beverage, tourism, technology, and entertainment.

**Key Results:** The analysis successfully identified significant trends and provided predictive insights into their emergence and growth. The methodology demonstrated its adaptability for use in other regions, emphasizing its robustness.

**Why It Matters:** By leveraging ongoing AI-powered trend analysis, businesses can make more informed, data-driven decisions in a dynamic market environment. This approach not only aids in recognizing opportunities for investment but also enhances strategic planning in alignment with the evolving economic landscape of Saudi Arabia.

### 3) [2502.16740v1] I am not thinking anymore, just following the path.: Investigating Task Delegation Trend of Author-AI Co-Creation with Generative AIs
https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.16740v1

**Summary:** **Problem:** The paper explores how digital comic authors delegate tasks to generative AIs during the creative process, particularly focusing on the balance between human input and AI assistance.

**Approach:** The authors observed 16 digital comic creators during the drafting stage, categorizing their levels of task delegation to AI. They analyzed the extent of this delegation, variations in AI usage, and how authors adjusted their delegation strategies throughout the co-creation process.

**Key Results:** Findings revealed that most authors significantly delegated tasks to AI, correlating higher delegation with reduced creation time and more detailed queries to AI. However, about 60% of authors later recalibrated their delegation levels, often opting for less reliance on AI due to concerns over losing creative agency and dissatisfaction with AI's originality.

**Why It Matters:** This study highlights the evolving dynamics of human-AI collaboration in creative fields, offering insights into effective task delegation strategies. It emphasizes the need to redefine trust in co-creation and proposes new metrics for assessing trust in human-AI interactions, which can inform future practices in creative industries.
