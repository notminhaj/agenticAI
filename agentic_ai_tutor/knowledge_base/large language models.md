# large language models

## Large Language Models (LLMs) - Overview

Large Language Models (LLMs) are AI systems designed to understand and generate human language and more, trained on billions of parameters from diverse datasets. They use neural networks, machine learning, and especially Transformer architecture (self-attention) to predict and generate text, enabling applications from chatbots to code generation.

### Key Points:
- LLMs can understand various forms of language including natural languages, code, symbols, and signs.
- Major models include GPT series, BERT, LaMDA, PaLM 2, and others.
- Developed rapidly from early chatbots (ELIZA, 1966) to advanced models like GPT-4 (2023).
- Capabilities: text generation, language translation, summarization, conversational agents, code generation, sentiment analysis, bug detection, and more.
- Challenges include hallucinations (making up info), biases in training data, scalability issues, and environmental impact of large training costs.
- The future points to democratization, improved productivity, and ethical considerations.

### Links to Current Understanding:
- Builds foundational knowledge supporting Agentic AI by providing natural language understanding and generation capabilities vital for autonomous AI agents.
- Complements Model Context Protocol by enabling richer language-based context handling and better multi-turn interactions.
